> "Are we just scared because we don't know what the migration will do or look like afterwards? Verify the fear instead of assuming."

**Verified.** Here's what's fear vs. fact:

### What you should NOT be scared of (verified)

Google treats the cutover as same-entity (same domain, schema preserved, NAP frozen). ### What you SHOULD be cautious about (verified)
- **Analytics black-out at flip moment.** The live WP site uses unified Google Tag `GT-NGS794C2` (Site Kit). The new Vercel site emits `GTM-TBCXCXGS` (a different/unused container). If we flip without swapping `build.py:153`, GA4 + Google Ads conversion data discontinues at flip — meaning you won't be able to *prove* the flip didn't hurt you. The site itself still works; you just go blind on attribution.

Most likely GBP already matches `BRAND` (Brock has not changed GBP since the WP site was built), in which case the cutover is a no-op for GBP. The screenshot capture is the proof, not the fix.

### The fear that IS real

- **Brock-only paperwork on the launch gate.** Even if everything underneath is fine, the gate stays HARD NO-GO until those 4 rows get Brock's handwriting.
