Common repair calls

  • Flickering lights on one circuit, the whole house, or only when an appliance kicks on.
  • Dead outlets — including outlets that work when first plugged in but stop after a few minutes.
  • Repeated breaker trips — usually overload, short circuit, or a failing breaker.
  • GFCI outlets that won't reset.
  • AFCI breakers tripping at random — common with arcing in old wiring or shared neutrals.
  • Buzzing switches or outlets that get warm with normal use.
  • Lights too dim or too bright — possible loose neutral, often dangerous if left alone.
  • Aluminum-branch wiring concerns in OKC-area homes built between roughly 1965 and 1975. .

FAQ

Common questions

Do you charge for diagnosis?

No. Diagnosis is free with any service call. We tell you what's wrong, what it'll cost to fix, and what the realistic options are — before any work begins.

Why do my breakers keep tripping?

Three usual suspects: an overloaded circuit (too many things drawing too much), a short circuit (hot wire touching neutral or ground), or a failing breaker. We can find which one in usually under an hour.

Should I worry about flickering lights?

Sometimes. If lights flicker only when one appliance starts up, that's usually normal. If they flicker randomly, dim throughout the house, or come with buzzing, get them checked — that pattern can indicate a loose service neutral, which is dangerous.

Do you work on aluminum wiring?

. Aluminum-branch wiring is common in OKC-area homes built 1965–1975 and has known fire-risk failure modes at terminations. Call us and we'll tell you whether it's a service we handle directly or refer.

Can you fix it today?

Most repair calls in the OKC metro can be same-day. Call and we'll tell you the actual schedule.
