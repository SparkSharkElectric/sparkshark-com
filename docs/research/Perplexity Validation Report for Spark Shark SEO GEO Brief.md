## 8. GEO / AI Search Corrections

### What the Document Gets Right
The answer-first structure, entity-clear prose, and FAQ formatting recommendations are sound GEO practices supported by current evidence. These should be kept as written.[^13][^12]

### llms.txt — Overclaimed
The document recommends llms.txt as "low cost, optional upside" and states it may help AI crawlers. The current evidence is substantially more negative than the document implies. A 2026 independent analysis found llms.txt appears in only 0.00105693% of AI-cited content, and SE Ranking's analysis of 300,000 domains found no statistically significant AI citation lift. Google has publicly stated its AI systems do not currently use the file. Wix's November 2025 research found evidence of indexing and some AI surface appearances, but this does not translate to citation improvement. **Recommendation:** Implement llms.txt as minimal hygiene (the implementation cost is ~30 minutes), but remove any language suggesting it meaningfully improves AI visibility. It is not a GEO strategy — it is a future-proofing file.[^49][^20]

### Entity Disambiguation Is the Real GEO Priority
The existence of Spark Shark LLC (Wisconsin) and Spark Shark Electric (Ontario, Canada) means AI engines may confuse or conflate these entities when answering "who is Spark Shark Electric?" questions. The single most important GEO move is consistent, unambiguous entity anchoring: every page says "Spark Shark Electric in Moore, Oklahoma" and all schema `sameAs` links point to Oklahoma-based profiles only.[^35][^33][^34]

### Multi-Platform Citation Presence
GEO research consistently finds that local businesses cited across review sites, social media, and forums are seen as more authoritative by AI systems. Beyond citations on sparkshark.com, Spark Shark should prioritize: (1) responding to all existing Google and Thumbtack reviews, (2) building a consistent presence on Nextdoor for Moore/OKC neighborhoods, (3) ensuring Bing Places, Apple Maps, and Angi listings match the canonical NAP.[^12]

### Review Response GEO Opportunity
Owner responses to reviews are indexed and crawled. When Brock or the team responds to a Google review and naturally includes "Thank you for letting Spark Shark Electric handle your panel upgrade in Moore, OK" — that creates an additional AI-indexable entity-service-city connection. This costs zero budget and compounds over time. However, after April 2026, responses should not include technician names (to avoid the new filtered-name review policy applying to reply content).[^31][^24]
