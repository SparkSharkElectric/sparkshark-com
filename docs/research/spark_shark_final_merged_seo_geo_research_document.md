6. **Same-day / 24/7 emergency** — every major competitor advertises 24/7. This is table stakes, not a differentiator.
7. **Veteran / military / first-responder discount** — Spark Shark, Barnett, and others all mention this.

## 5. Spark Shark Positioning Strategy

Spark Shark's defensible angle without overclaiming:

1. **Residential-only focus.** Most direct competitors (Barnett, Bliss, Mr. Electric, Edge) split between residential, commercial, industrial. Spark Shark serves homes only — more relevant expertise for homeowner needs (older OKC wiring, panel upgrades, EV chargers, smart-home retrofits, generator backups for Oklahoma weather). Lead with this.
2. **Modern, locally based, Moore-headquartered.** Founded 2021 by Founder and CEO Brock Flanary. The "young company, new playbook" angle — flat-rate written pricing, transparent communication, modern dispatch software — vs. legacy competitors' decades-old systems. Position as the *modern* Moore electrician, not the *oldest*.
3. **Honest, written, flat-rate pricing.** This is on the live site already. Lean on it. Frame against competitor patterns where customers report sticker shock from franchise brands ("$1,118 quote vs. $450 from independent" — real complaint pattern in Mister Sparky OKC reviews).
4. **24/7 live response** for true emergencies (not "AI agent" — real dispatch). Frame around Oklahoma weather realities: ice storms, severe storms, tornado-related electrical damage.
5. **Generac generator capability** for Oklahoma's outage-heavy climate — but do NOT position as a generator specialist (that lane belongs to Snyder Electric). Position generators as one strong vertical inside a full residential menu.
6. **Servant/community framing** — military and first-responder discounts, local Moore HQ at 621 Sally Ct, led by Founder and CEO Brock Flanary. Authentic vs. franchise.
7. **Brand clarity.** "Spark Shark" is memorable, ownable, and visual. Most competitors have generic names ("Edge Electric," "Bliss Electric"). Use the brand and the shark mark consistently — it's a real asset.

**Do not position on:** lowest price, most reviewed, longest tenure, biggest team, "the best."

**Anti-positioning:** Spark Shark is residential-focused, locally led, flat-rate, and modern — the alternative to franchise upsell pressure on one side and aging legacy shops on the other.

Spark Shark should not sound like “another electrician.” The strategic narrative is:

> **Spark Shark Electric is the modern, residential-only electrical company for OKC metro homeowners who want clear pricing, clean work, licensed accountability, and a service experience that does not feel like an old-school contractor or a national franchise machine.**

### 6A.2 Homeowner psychology and search intent

Gemini emphasized that electrical search intent is usually **transactional**, not casual. A homeowner searching for an electrician is often trying to solve risk, fear, or uncertainty.

| Search intent | Example searches | Psychological state | Copy priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Urgent crisis | “burning smell outlet,” “sparking outlet,” “power out electrician” | Anxiety / urgency | Phone-first CTA, reassurance, licensed dispatch, no AI language. |
| Planned upgrade | “panel upgrade cost OKC,” “generator installation Moore” | Research / comparison | Clear process, written options, financing placeholder, proof of expertise. |
| Trust verification | “Spark Shark Electric reviews,” “licensed electrician Moore” | Skepticism | Reviews, BBB, license, real photos, clear NAP. |
| Local convenience | “electrician near me,” “Moore electrician” | Speed / proximity | Moore base, city pages, service-area proof, GBP consistency. |

Copy should systematically remove these homeowner fears:

1. Fear of being scammed.
2. Fear of surprise pricing.
3. Fear that the electrician will not show up.
4. Fear of unsafe or sloppy work.
5. Fear of being pressured into a large replacement.
6. Fear that an emergency will be routed to a robot instead of a person.

## 7. Page-Type Copy Rules

> Conventions used below:
> - **Word count** is total page body copy excluding nav/footer.
> - **AI-extractable** = a 40–60 word answer-paragraph in the first 300 words of the page that directly answers the page's core question, in declarative present-tense prose with the city name and "Spark Shark Electric" as the entity.
> - **Internal linking targets** are URLs the page must link to.
> - All schema must validate at validator.schema.org and search.google.com/test/rich-results.

### 7.1 Homepage (`/`)
- **H1 pattern:** `Residential Electrician in Moore & the Oklahoma City Metro`
- **Intro paragraph (first 60 words, AI-extractable):** Open with: *"Spark Shark Electric is a licensed residential electrical contractor based in Moore, Oklahoma, serving homeowners across the Oklahoma City metro since 2021. We handle electrical repairs, panel upgrades, generator installations, EV chargers, lighting, and 24/7 emergencies — all on flat-rate, written pricing."* Then the value prop and CTAs.
- **Required sections (in order):**
  1. Hero (H1, sub-headline, primary CTA, phone, sticky on mobile)
  2. Trust strip (license #, BBB-accredited badge, years in business, residential-only)
  3. Core services grid (12–13 service tiles linking to service pages)
  4. "Why Spark Shark" — 3–4 differentiators (residential focus, flat-rate written quotes, licensed/bonded/insured, 24/7 dispatch)
  5. Service area (city links — 10 city pages)
  6. Reviews/testimonials (real names + neighborhoods if possible). Use AggregateRating schema only if review count is verified.
  7. FAQ (5–7 Q&As, marked up with FAQPage schema)
  8. Final CTA + map
- **Internal linking:** every named service to its service page; every named city to its city page; About; Reviews; Contact; FAQ.
- **Schema:** `Electrician` (subtype of LocalBusiness), `BreadcrumbList`, `FAQPage`, optional `AggregateRating` (only with verified counts).

## 12. GEO / AI Answer-Extraction Rules

Modern AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini) cite content that is structured, declarative, and entity-clear. Optimize accordingly.

### Answer-first structure
- Every page's first 60 words must contain a direct, declarative answer to the page's core question.
- Format: **`[Entity] [verb] [what] [where]`** — e.g., *"Spark Shark Electric installs whole-home Generac generators in Moore and Oklahoma City."*
- Avoid burying the answer under marketing fluff. AI extractors cite the first clean answer.

### Extractable paragraph format
Place these "answer capsules" throughout content:
- 40–80 words long
- 1 declarative sentence stating the fact, followed by 1–2 supporting sentences with specifics (cities, license, services, phone).
- Always name the entity ("Spark Shark Electric") in the answer capsule, not just pronouns.
- Always name at least 1 city.

### Entity clarity
- Use the full brand name "Spark Shark Electric" on first mention per page (not just "Spark Shark" or "we").
- Repeat the entity name 4–6 times per 1,000 words naturally.
- Always pair entity with location ("Spark Shark Electric in Moore, OK").
- Use `sameAs` schema linking to: Google Business Profile URL, BBB profile, Yelp, Facebook, LinkedIn (Brock Flanary), ProvenExpert.

### NAP consistency across the web
Audit and fix listings on: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Maps, BBB, Yelp, Facebook, Yellow Pages, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Networx, Nextdoor, Houzz, Thumbtack, Manta, Foursquare, MapQuest. AI engines triangulate identity — inconsistent NAP suppresses citations.
