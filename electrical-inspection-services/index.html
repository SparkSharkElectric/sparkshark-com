An electrical inspection is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy, and it's included free with any paid service call.

When you need an inspection

  • Buying or selling a home. Pre-purchase electrical inspections often surface issues that the general home inspector flagged but didn't dig into.
  • Before a renovation. Know what your panel can support before you spec the new kitchen, addition, or finished basement.
  • Your insurance carrier required it. Carriers increasingly ask for one when the home is over 40 years old or has a Federal Pacific / Zinsco / aluminum-branch panel.
  • After a flood, fire, or storm. Even if everything looks normal, the wiring or panel may have taken damage.
  • Adding a major load: EV charger, hot tub, electric range, whole-home generator.
  • Just to know. Free with any paid service call.

What we check

  • Service entry, meter base, and main panel — including a thermal scan to find hot spots before they fail.
  • All accessible sub-panels.
  • GFCI and AFCI protection in code-required areas (kitchens, baths, garages, exterior, bedrooms).
  • Smoke and CO detector placement and age.
  • Sample of outlets and switches in every room — for polarity, grounding, and tightness.
  • Visible junction boxes for proper covers and connections.
  • Grounding system (rod, water-pipe bond, panel bond).
  • Service disconnect labeling and accessibility.

What does a home electrical inspection include in Oklahoma?

  • A written walkthrough of the items above, with photos of issues found.
  • Plain-language explanation of which items are urgent, which are recommended, and which are routine.
  • A flat-rate written quote on any work you decide to schedule.
  • Free with any paid service call.
FAQ

Common questions

How long does an inspection take?

Most homes take 60–90 minutes for the full inspection. Larger homes or homes with multiple sub-panels can run 2 hours.

Will you give me a written report?

Yes. Every inspection includes a written report you can hand to a buyer, seller, insurance carrier, or contractor.

Do you fix what you find on the same visit?

Often yes for small issues. Larger issues get a separate written quote so you can decide what to address.

Is the inspection really free with a service call?

Yes. While we're at your house for any other paid service, we'll do a complete safety inspection at no additional charge.

Will my insurance carrier accept your report?

Most insurance carriers accept a written safety inspection from a licensed Oklahoma electrical contractor. If your carrier has a specific form, we'll complete it.
