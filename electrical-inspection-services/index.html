An electrical inspection is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy. We walk every panel, every accessible junction, every outlet sample, every service entry, and every safety device. You get a written report you can hand to a buyer, an insurer, or your contractor.

When you need an inspection

  • Buying or selling a home. Pre-purchase electrical inspections often find issues that the general home inspector flagged but didn't dig into.
  • Before a renovation. Know what your panel can support before you spec the new kitchen.
  • Insurance carrier required it. Carriers increasingly ask for one when the home is over 40 years old or has a Federal Pacific / Zinsco / aluminum-branch panel.
  • After a flood, fire, or storm. Even if everything looks normal, the wiring or panel may have taken damage.
  • Just to know. Free with any service call.

What we check

  • Service entry, meter base, and main panel — including a thermal scan to find hot spots before they fail.
  • All accessible sub-panels.
  • GFCI and AFCI protection in code-required areas (kitchens, baths, garages, exterior, bedrooms).
  • Smoke and CO detector placement and age.
  • Sample of outlets and switches in every room — for polarity, grounding, and tightness.
  • Visible junction boxes for proper covers and connections.
  • Grounding system (rod, water-pipe bond, panel bond).
  • Service disconnect labeling and accessibility.
FAQ

Common questions

How long does an inspection take?

Most homes take 60–90 minutes for the full inspection. Larger homes or homes with multiple sub-panels can run 2 hours.

Will you give me a written report?

Yes — every inspection includes a written report you can hand to a buyer, seller, insurance carrier, or contractor.

Do you fix what you find on the same visit?

Often yes for small issues. Larger issues get a separate written quote so you can decide what to address.

Is the inspection really free with a service call?

Yes. While we're at your house for any other service, we'll do a complete safety inspection at no additional charge.
