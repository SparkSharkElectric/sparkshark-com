Call right now if you have any of these

  • Burning smell from any outlet, switch, or the panel itself.
  • Visible smoke or scorching at a panel, outlet, or fixture.
  • Hot to the touch — outlet covers, switch plates, or the panel cover warmer than ambient.
  • Buzzing or popping from a panel or outlet.
  • Sparking outlets beyond a quick blue flash when plugging in.
  • Partial power loss — half the house out, or one room dead while breakers look fine.
  • Repeated breaker trips on the same circuit, especially after storms or flooding.
  • Storm damage to your service mast, meter base, or weatherhead after Oklahoma's seasonal storms.

Stop. Don't do these.

  • Don't reset a breaker that just tripped if you smell burning — find the cause first.
  • Don't pour water on an electrical fire — kill the panel main first if you can do so safely, then use a Class C extinguisher.
  • Don't probe an outlet or switch with anything metal.
  • Don't run a generator backfeed into your house through an outlet — that's a line-worker fatality risk and a fire risk.

What you can do while we're on the way

  • If safe, flip the main breaker off at the panel.
  • Unplug major loads from the affected circuit (heaters, dryers, A/C window units).
  • If the smell is strong or there's smoke, leave the house and call from outside.
  • If you suspect the panel itself, do not stand directly in front of it; arc events can cause flash burns.

FAQ

Common questions

Is there an extra charge for nights and weekends?

We answer 24/7 and dispatch a licensed electrician as soon as one is available. Pricing is flat-rate based on the work and written before we start. — we'll be transparent about any after-hours rate when you call.

How fast can you get here?

We dispatch as fast as the on-call electrician can arrive. When you call, we'll tell you the actual ETA based on who's closest and the nature of your emergency. We don't promise specific arrival times we can't keep.

Can you talk me through the situation on the phone?

Yes. If something feels off, call. We'd rather answer the phone, walk through what's happening, and figure out together whether it's an emergency, a same-day call, or a next-day appointment.

Do you serve commercial buildings?

No — Spark Shark Electric is a residential-only electrical contractor. For commercial emergencies we can refer you to a trusted commercial electrician.

Do you handle storm damage to the service mast or meter base?

Yes. After Oklahoma storms, we handle service mast repair, meter base replacement, and the OG&E coordination required to restore power safely.
