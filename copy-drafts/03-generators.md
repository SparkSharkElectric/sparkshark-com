=== INTRO PARAGRAPH (lede paragraph at top of body) ===
Spark Shark Electric in Moore, Oklahoma installs whole-home Generac standby generators for OKC-metro homeowners. Oklahoma weather doesn't ask permission — ice storms, line-of-thunderstorms, tornadic systems, and prolonged grid outages. A whole-home standby generator powered by natural gas or LP starts within seconds of a power loss and runs your house until the grid comes back. We size it, install the automatic transfer switch, pull the permits, and commission the system end-to-end.

=== SECTION: What we install ===
- **Whole-home Generac standby generators**: natural gas or LP, sized to your load (commonly 14kW–26kW for OKC-metro homes).
- **Automatic transfer switches (ATS)** sense the outage and start the generator within seconds.
- **Sub-panels for managed loads**: optional, for homes where running everything at once would oversize the unit.
- **Wi-Fi monitoring**: see status and run history from your phone.
- **Annual maintenance**: oil change, filter, exercise-mode test, transfer-switch verification.

=== SECTION: Why a standby generator in Oklahoma ===
- **Ice storms** routinely take down lines across the OKC metro for days at a time.
- **Severe and tornadic storms** cause localized outages that aren't always priority restoration zones.
- **Summer heat advisories** put grid load at peak, and outages during a heat event are a medical risk for anyone with health vulnerabilities or small children at home.
- **Whole-home backup** keeps HVAC, refrigeration, well pumps, sump pumps, internet, and medical equipment running without anyone touching anything.

=== SECTION: How we size a generator ===
- We look at your service size, the breakers you actually use, and which loads matter when the power's out.
- **Critical loads** (HVAC, fridge/freezer, kitchen, lighting, internet) come first.
- **Optional loads** (electric vehicle charging, pool equipment, large workshops) are layered in or load-managed.
- We don't oversell. A too-large generator costs more, uses more fuel, and runs less efficiently.

=== SECTION: How the install runs ===
- **Day one:** generator pad set, gas line tied in by a licensed plumber (we coordinate), ATS wired into your panel.
- **Day two:** commissioning (load-bank test, ATS dry-run, automatic transfer test, manual override test).
- **Same day as commissioning:** walkthrough on monitoring, exercise schedule, and what to expect during the next outage.
- **Permit pulled on every install.** Inspection scheduled.

=== SECTION: Generators are part of a complete residential electrical system ===
- A generator install often pairs with a panel upgrade. Spark Shark Electric handles both, and we'll cross-quote the panel and the generator in a single written estimate so you can plan the project once.
- Annual maintenance keeps the unit ready for the day it actually has to run. We offer maintenance plans on units we install.

=== FAQ ===
Q: How long can a standby generator run?
A: On natural gas, indefinitely — as long as utility gas keeps flowing, the generator runs. On LP, runtime depends on tank size; a 500-gallon tank typically supports 5–10 days of full-load operation.

Q: Do I need a permit?
A: Yes. Generator installs require both an electrical permit and (typically) a gas permit. Both are pulled by us as part of the job.

Q: How loud is it?
A: Modern enclosed standby generators are roughly 65–70 dB at 23 feet, about as loud as a window A/C unit. Setback distances from windows are dictated by code; we handle siting.

Q: How much does a whole-home generator install cost in Moore or OKC?
A: It depends on generator size, gas-line work, ATS complexity, panel modifications, and any pad/site prep needed. After a free site visit, we give you a written flat-rate quote, with no hidden fees and no add-ons after the fact.

Q: Do I need to upgrade my electrical panel before installing a generator?
A: Sometimes. Older or undersized panels often need a service upgrade to support a whole-home generator. We assess your panel during the site visit and bundle any required work into one written quote.
