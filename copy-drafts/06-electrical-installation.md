=== SECTION: What we install ===
- **New circuits** for kitchens, garages, workshops, and home offices.
- **Whole-home rewires** for older homes with knob-and-tube or aluminum branch wiring.
- **Smart-home wiring** — structured cable, in-wall A/V, smart switches and dimmers.
- **EV charger circuits** — Level 2 (240V) home stations.
- **Ceiling fans** — including new boxes and switching for rooms that don't have them.
- **Outdoor lighting** — landscape, security, soffit, and patio.
- **Indoor lighting** — recessed cans, under-cabinet, pendants, dimmers.
- **Hot tub, sauna, and pool circuits** — dedicated GFCI-protected circuits with proper disconnects.

=== SECTION: How an install runs ===
- **Site visit.** We look at your existing panel and where you want the work, talk through what you actually need.
- **Written estimate.** Flat-rate. Permit fee included where a permit applies.
- **Schedule.** Most single-circuit installs are 2–4 hours; rewires are days to a week depending on home size and access.
- **Inspection** if applicable. **Cleanup** at the end of every day.

=== FAQ ===
Q: Do new outlets need a permit?
A: A single new outlet typically doesn't, but a new circuit usually does. We always pull the permit when one applies — it's part of our flat rate.

Q: Can you wire a hot tub?
A: Yes — including the dedicated 50A or 60A GFCI-protected circuit and the disconnect at the equipment.

Q: Can you install the EV charger I bought online?
A: Yes. We wire the dedicated circuit, install any UL-listed wall-mount station per the manufacturer's instructions, and commission it.

Q: Do you do new construction?
A: We focus on residential remodels and existing-home installs. For ground-up new construction we partner with a few preferred general contractors.

Q: How long does a whole-home rewire take?
A: It depends on home size, access (attic/crawlspace), and how much drywall the customer is willing to open. Most rewires run 3–7 days. We'll give you a realistic schedule before we start.
