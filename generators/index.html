Oklahoma weather doesn't ask permission. Ice storms, line-of-thunderstorms, tornadic systems — outages happen. A whole-home standby generator powered by natural gas or LP starts within seconds of a power loss and runs your house until the grid comes back. We size it, install it, permit it, and commission it.

What we install

  • Whole-home standby generators — natural gas or LP, sized to your load (typically 14kW–26kW for most OKC homes).
  • Automatic transfer switches (ATS) — sense the outage and start the generator within 10 seconds.
  • Sub-panels for managed loads — optional, for homes where running everything at once would oversize the unit.
  • Wi-Fi monitoring — see status and run history from your phone.
  • Annual maintenance — oil change, filter, exercise mode test, transfer switch verification.

How we size a generator

  • We look at your service size, the breakers you actually use, and which loads matter when the power's out.
  • Critical loads (HVAC, fridge/freezer, kitchen, lighting, internet) come first.
  • Optional loads (electric vehicle charging, pool equipment, large workshops) are layered in or load-managed.
  • We don't oversell — a too-large generator costs more, uses more fuel, and runs less efficiently.

How the install runs

  • Day one: generator pad set, gas line tied in by a licensed plumber (we coordinate), ATS wired into your panel.
  • Day two: commissioning — load-bank test, ATS dry-run, automatic transfer test, manual override test.
  • Same day: walkthrough on monitoring, exercise schedule, and what to expect during the next outage.
  • Permit pulled on every install. Inspection scheduled.
FAQ

Common questions

How long can a standby generator run?

On natural gas, indefinitely — as long as utility gas keeps flowing, the generator runs. On LP, runtime depends on tank size; a 500-gallon tank typically supports 5–10 days of full-load operation.

Do I need a permit?

Yes — generator installs require both an electrical permit and (typically) a gas permit. Both are pulled by us as part of the job.

How loud is it?

Modern enclosed standby generators are roughly 65–70 dB at 23 feet — about as loud as a window A/C unit. Setback distances from windows are dictated by code; we handle siting.

How much does it cost?

Whole-home installs in the OKC metro generally run $9,000–$18,000 turnkey, depending on generator size, gas-line work, ATS complexity, and any panel modifications. We give a flat-rate written quote after a free site visit.
